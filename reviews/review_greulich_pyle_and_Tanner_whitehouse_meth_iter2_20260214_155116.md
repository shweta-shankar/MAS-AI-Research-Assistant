GP method, first introduced by Eugene M. Greulich and Stewart Pyle in 1950, is a graphical representation of bone age that uses radiographs to determine the degree of skeletal maturity. The method involves measuring the epiphyseal closure and cartilaginous growth plate fusion in the long bones of the hand and wrist (Greulich & Pyle, 1950). However, GP has several limitations, including high inter-observer variability and subjectivity.

TW method, developed by John M.

Q3:How were the segmental GP labels assigned to each image?
Ans:Keywords: ['GP', ' labels', ' image', ' segmental']
Unique results: 248

Answer:
The segmental GP labels were assigned to each image by first segmenting the carpals and wrist region of the X-ray images using two separate segmentation models with a Unet backbone [README.md:31]. These models were trained on an open-access dataset and fine-tuned on an Indian dataset. The carpal and wrist crops were then generated and subtracted from the full-hand image to obtain the shortbones crop [README.md:31]. The bone age prediction models, which have a densenet161 backbone, were trained independently for each segment (full-hand, shortbones, carpals, and wrist) [README.md:49]. The segmental predictions were then equally weighted and rounded off to their nearest GP class to assign the segmental GP labels [README.md:49]. This process is part of the Segmental Greulich-Pyle (SGP) method, which takes into account the maturation variation of different segments of the hand to report a more appropriate bone age [README.md:18].
